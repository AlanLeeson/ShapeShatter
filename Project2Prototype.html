Shape Shater

High Concept

'Geometry Wars' meets 'Cut the Rope' for a fast paced screen clearer.

Genre

  • Twitch
  • Arcade
  • High Score
  • Endless waves

Platform

Mobile/Desktop

Story

None.

Aesthetics

Graphics Style - Biodigital Jazz, that's heavy on particle effects.

Sound - space effects, buzzing, techno background.

Where sound will play:

  • When a shape gets destroyed.
  • When the rope moves.
  • When shapes spawn.
  • When selecting a certain anchor.
  • Theme song.
  • Game States.

Gameplay

Mechanics

The player can click on one of the shapes at either ends of a rope to start controlling the rope. The player can swing the rope around the screen by moving their finger. Shapes will spawn at the corners of the screen that will damage the player if they collide with the shapes at the end of the player's rope. The player's objective is to destroy the opposing shapes by making them collide with the rope section between the two shape ends. The screen does not move and enemies will spawn infinitely in waves.

Controls

One Finger Press - Moves an anchor which enacts a force on the opposite anchor.

Player Learning

The first thing the player needs to learn is how to control the string and two anchors. The player should try pressing either the square or the triangle on the screen. When pressed, the other anchor will suddenly be affected by physics and the user will see the anchor begin to fall. When the player moves their finger, they will see that the anchor they are holding follows the finger and the rope between the two, pulls the other anchor along in a swinging motion. From this point the player can play the game and will learn through trial and error. When the rope collides with enemies, they earn points but if an anchor hits an enemy then the round is reset. The player will fail very quickly but with no penalty because the round can be immediately restarted. The last thing the player needs to learn is that when they let go of an anchor, the rope freezes and physics no longer affects the other anchor. All the enemies stop moving as well. This will teach the player that the game is essentially paused when a finger is not on an anchor. This allows the player time to think about avoiding enemies or how to get the highest combo. The player can then press on either anchor to enact a force on the opposite one and continue the game.
